Wiki - Spawners


Spawner GUI

Every Spawner includes its own management interface, giving you quick access to everything related to your mob farm.
The Chest is the main storage, where every item dropped by your mobs is collected automatically.
The Mob Head acts as a convenient shortcut to instantly sell every stored item without opening the storage first.
You will also find the Experience storage, allowing you to collect all accumulated XP whenever you want. This experience can also be used to repair equipment enchanted with Mending, making your farm even more efficient.


Storage

The Storage menu includes a variety of management tools designed to help you organize, filter, collect, sell, and control every item produced by your spawner.
The Cauldron automatically sorts the stored items, making your storage much easier to organize.
The Hopper opens the loot filter, allowing you to enable or disable specific mob drops.
The Gold Ingot instantly sells every item currently stored inside the spawner.
The Bundle transfers all stored items directly into your inventory.
Finally, the Dropper drops every stored item onto the ground, or only the current storage page if your storage spans multiple pages.


Loot Filter

The Loot Filter allows you to choose exactly which drops should be stored by your spawner.
It is divided into two categories: Allowed and Disallowed.
Any item moved to the Disallowed category will no longer appear in the spawner's storage, helping you keep only the drops you actually want.
Switching an item between the two categories is as simple as clicking on it.
Items with durability, such as tools, weapons, and armor, are automatically grouped together regardless of their durability value, making the filter easy to configure and maintain.


Stacking

Spawners can be stacked together to save space and increase the efficiency of your mob farms.
To add spawners to an existing stack, simply right-click the spawner. A normal right-click adds one spawner, while Shift + Right-Click adds as many spawners as possible from your inventory in a single action.
To remove spawners from a stack, use Left-Click. A normal left-click removes one spawner, while Shift + Left-Click removes an entire stack at once, making it much faster to relocate or reorganize your farms.
Each spawner stack can contain up to 10,000 spawners, allowing you to build extremely compact and efficient mob farms while saving valuable space.
Unlike vanilla Minecraft, Silk Touch is not required to break and recover spawners, allowing you to move and manage them freely whenever needed.
